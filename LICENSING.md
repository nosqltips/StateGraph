- Self-host AgentStateGraph on your own infrastructure

### You CANNOT (without a commercial license):
- Offer AgentStateGraph itself as a commercial managed service (e.g., "AgentStateGraph-as-a-Service" where the primary value is access to AgentStateGraph's features)

### The one restriction, explained
The only thing BSL 1.1 restricts is offering AgentStateGraph as a competing hosted service. This means a cloud provider cannot take the code, host it, and sell "Managed AgentStateGraph" as a product. This protection exists because without it, hyperscale cloud providers can (and historically do) take successful open-source infrastructure, host it as a managed service, and capture the commercial value without contributing back.

If you are building an application that uses AgentStateGraph as a component — even a critical one — you are not offering AgentStateGraph as a managed service. You're fine. If you're unsure whether your use case qualifies, contact us at craig@agentstategraph.dev.

## Automatic conversion to Apache 2.0

Every version of AgentStateGraph automatically converts to the **Apache License 2.0** four years after its release date. This means:

- **v0.3.5-beta.2** (released 2026-04-09) becomes Apache 2.0 on **2030-04-09**
- Future versions follow the same pattern

This guarantees that the code is eventually fully open source under a permissive license, while protecting the project's sustainability during the critical early years.

## Why BSL?

AgentStateGraph is a new infrastructure primitive. Building and maintaining it requires sustained investment — in the core engine, language bindings, storage backends, MCP server, documentation, security patches, and community support. The BSL model has been proven by CockroachDB, Sentry, HashiCorp, MariaDB, and others to sustain open-source infrastructure projects while preventing the well-documented pattern of hyperscale cloud providers strip-mining open-source value.

We chose BSL 1.1 specifically because:
- It's the most battle-tested source-available license for infrastructure software
- The automatic Apache 2.0 conversion gives the community a permanent guarantee
- End users, enterprises, and integrators are completely unaffected
- It's clear, readable, and has well-understood legal precedent
